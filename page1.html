Understanding GPA/CGPA

What is GPA/CGPA?

GPA (Grade Point Average) is the average of grades earned in courses over a term. CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the overall average of GPA over multiple terms or an academic program.

Grade Sheets Information:

After results are declared, Grade Sheets will be issued to each student which will contain the following details:

  • The college in which the candidate has studied
  • The list of courses enrolled during the semester and the grade scored
  • The Grade Point Average (GPA) for the semester
  • The Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) of all courses enrolled from the first semester onwards

How to Calculate GPA/CGPA?

GPA for a semester is the ratio of the sum of the products of the number of credits for courses acquired and the corresponding points to the sum of the number of credits for the courses acquired in the semester. CGPA will be calculated similarly, considering all the courses registered from the first semester. U grades will be excluded for calculating GPA and CGPA.

GPA Formula:
GPA = ∑ (Grade Points × Course Credits) / ∑Course Credits

CGPA Formula:
CGPA = ∑ (GPA for each term × Credits for that term) / ∑ Total Credits for all terms
